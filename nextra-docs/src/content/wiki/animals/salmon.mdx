# Salmon

Witness one of nature's most incredible journeys right in your Minecraft world. Salmon are remarkable fish that undertake epic migrations, swimming against powerful currents and leaping up waterfalls to reach their destination. These silvery swimmers bring the drama of the wild ocean to your rivers and coastlines.

## What Makes Salmon Special?

### The Great Migration

Salmon are born with an incredible instinct - they must swim upstream! Watch closely and you'll see salmon actively fighting against river currents, determinedly heading toward the source of the water. This isn't random wandering - it's a purposeful journey hardcoded into their very nature.

**Did you know?** In the real world, salmon migrate hundreds of miles upstream to spawn. In Better Ecology, you can witness this same ancient drive as they navigate your river systems!

### Masters of the Leap

Here's where salmon truly shine - they can jump up waterfalls! When a salmon encounters flowing water dropping down, it doesn't turn back. Instead, it gathers its strength and launches itself upward, attempting to clear the obstacle.

Stand near a waterfall in a cold river and watch for these spectacular leaps. You might see several salmon attempting the same jump, one after another, as they work their way upstream. It's one of the most dramatic natural behaviors you can observe in Minecraft.

**Try this:** Build a multi-level waterfall in a cold river biome and watch salmon navigate it. They'll work their way up level by level, jumping each cascade. Some might need multiple attempts!

### Swimming in Schools

Safety in numbers isn't just a saying - it's survival strategy for salmon. These fish naturally gather into schools, swimming together in coordinated groups. You'll rarely see a lone salmon for long; they seek out others of their kind.

Watch a school move through the water and you'll notice they maintain formation, turning and moving as a unified group. When one salmon changes direction, others nearby often follow, creating beautiful flowing patterns as they navigate river bends and ocean currents.

**Tip:** Schools of 5-8 salmon create the most impressive displays. Look for these larger groups in ocean biomes and at river mouths where multiple salmon gather before beginning their upstream journey.

## Life in the Water

### Where to Find Them

Salmon have specific habitat preferences - they're creatures of cold water. You'll find them in:

**Cold Ocean Biomes:** Large schools swimming near the surface, moving between kelp forests and open water. This is where young salmon spend much of their time.

**Rivers (especially cold rivers):** Smaller groups pushing upstream against the current. The colder the river, the more salmon you're likely to find.

**River Mouths:** The junction between ocean and river is prime salmon territory. You might see schools gathering here, preparing to begin their upstream migration.

**Deep Ocean Waters:** While less common, salmon occasionally venture into deeper ocean zones, though they prefer to stay in colder waters closer to shore.

### Size Variations

Not all salmon are created equal! You'll encounter different sizes as you explore:

**Small Salmon:** Young fish, still learning the ways of the water. These little swimmers stick especially close to their schools.

**Medium Salmon:** The most common size - healthy adult fish in their prime.

**Large Salmon:** Impressive specimens that have survived many challenges. These veterans often lead schools and are the first to attempt difficult waterfall jumps.

**Did you know?** Larger salmon can make slightly higher jumps when navigating waterfalls. Watch for the big ones to succeed where smaller fish struggle!

### Daily Patterns

Salmon are active creatures at all hours, but you'll notice certain patterns:

**Daytime:** Schools are most visible, swimming near the surface where you can easily observe their movements. Perfect time for salmon watching!

**Ocean Behavior:** More spread out, schools roam between different areas, feeding and growing stronger before their river journey.

**River Behavior:** Focused and purposeful - these salmon are on a mission. They'll rest in calmer pools but continue pushing upstream whenever possible.

**Night:** Salmon remain active but may swim slightly deeper. Schools stay together even in darkness.

## Predators and Survival

### Constant Vigilance

Life isn't easy for salmon. They face numerous threats, and they know it. You'll notice salmon are always alert, ready to scatter at the first sign of danger.

**Bears:** The salmon's primary natural enemy. When a bear appears near a river, watch how quickly salmon react - they'll dart away to safer waters. Bears are patient hunters, though, and will wait for the perfect moment to strike.

**Other Predators:** Ocelots, foxes, and other hunters also pose threats, especially in shallow water where salmon have less room to maneuver.

**Try this:** Observe a river with both salmon and a bear. You'll witness a tense game of cat-and-mouse as salmon try to navigate upstream while avoiding becoming a meal. It's nature documentary material!

### Escape Tactics

When threatened, salmon don't just sit still. They employ several survival strategies:

- **Burst Swimming:** Sudden bursts of speed to create distance from predators
- **School Scattering:** The school explodes in different directions, confusing attackers
- **Deep Diving:** Heading to deeper water where some predators can't follow
- **Current Riding:** Using water flow to quickly move away from danger

**Did you know?** A salmon's best defense is its school. Predators have a harder time targeting one fish among many, so staying grouped improves everyone's chances of survival.

## Important Food Source

### Part of the Ecosystem

Salmon aren't just interesting to watch - they're a crucial part of the food chain. They provide:

**Nutrition for Predators:** Bears especially rely on salmon as a primary food source. You might see well-fed bears near productive salmon runs.

**Food for Players:** Raw salmon can be cooked for a reliable food source, or traded with villagers.

**Bone Meal:** When processed, salmon yield bone meal - useful for players looking to accelerate crop growth.

**Tip:** If you're planning a bear habitat or wildlife preserve, make sure to include a river with salmon. The bears will naturally hunt them, creating authentic ecosystem interactions.

## Observing Salmon Behavior

### The Perfect Viewing Spot

Want to really appreciate salmon? Build yourself an underwater viewing chamber or a riverside observation platform. Here's what to look for:

**School Coordination:** Watch how fish maintain spacing and mirror each other's movements. When one turns, others adjust to maintain formation.

**Jump Attempts:** Some salmon make it on the first try, others need several attempts. You might see the same fish try multiple times to clear a waterfall.

**Current Fighting:** See how salmon angle their bodies to push through flowing water, making steady progress against the stream.

**Size Sorting:** Sometimes schools organize with similar-sized fish together, though they'll mix with other sizes too.

### Migration Routes

Track where salmon go! You might discover:

- Popular jumping spots where many salmon attempt the same waterfall
- Resting pools where tired salmon gather before the next leg of their journey
- Main migration corridors through your river system
- Seasonal patterns in ocean-to-river movement

**Try this:** Mark a salmon with name tags (if you can catch them) and track which route they take upstream. You might find they follow similar paths through your river system.

## Living Alongside Salmon

### Conservation Tips

Want to maintain a healthy salmon population? Consider these approaches:

**Preserve Natural Rivers:** Avoid damming or dramatically altering river courses. Salmon need flowing water for their migrations.

**Create Fish Ladders:** If you must build across a river, include stepped pools or gradual slopes that salmon can navigate.

**Protect Spawning Areas:** Keep upstream river sections pristine - these are crucial for salmon completing their journey.

**Limit Predator Pressure:** While predation is natural, an overabundance of bears in one river can deplete salmon populations. Balance is key!

### Fishing Strategies

If you're looking to catch salmon (rather than just observe them):

**River Mouths:** High concentration of fish entering freshwater - prime fishing spots.

**Pools Below Waterfalls:** Exhausted salmon rest here before attempting jumps - they're less likely to escape quickly.

**Ocean Schools:** Cast your line into a visible school for the best chances.

**Tip:** Don't overfish a single area. Salmon populations need time to recover, and you'll want them around for future fishing and viewing!

## Common Questions

**Q: Why do salmon keep swimming against the current?**
A: That's their natural migration behavior! Salmon are hardwired to swim upstream. It's not a bug - it's one of nature's most amazing instincts brought to life in Better Ecology.

**Q: Can salmon survive jumping any height waterfall?**
A: Salmon can attempt impressive jumps, but extremely tall waterfalls (4+ blocks) become very challenging. They're persistent, though - you might see them make many attempts!

**Q: Do salmon ever swim downstream?**
A: While their primary drive is upstream, salmon will move around locally and may drift downstream if resting or fleeing predators. But they'll always try to resume their upstream journey.

**Q: How do I keep salmon in one area?**
A: That's tricky because of their migration instinct! You'd need to create a large enclosed body of water without strong currents. Even then, salmon will naturally try to swim against any flow they detect.

**Q: Will salmon jump out of water onto land?**
A: Salmon jump specifically to clear waterfalls and obstacles in rivers. They won't randomly beach themselves - their jumps are purposeful and directed at continuing upstream.

**Q: Are salmon active all year?**
A: Yes! Unlike real-world salmon that have specific spawning seasons, Minecraft salmon are active year-round, constantly migrating and living their aquatic lives.
