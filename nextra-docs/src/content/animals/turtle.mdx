## Behaviors

### Beach Nesting

Turtles return to their home beach to lay eggs.

- Remember home beach location
- Navigate across ocean to return
- Lay eggs only on home beach sand
- Dig nest for eggs

### Egg Protection

Adult turtles near eggs show protective behavior.

- Stay near nest area
- Increased vigilance
- Defend against egg predators

### Ocean Navigation

Turtles navigate effectively in ocean biomes.

- Long-distance swimming
- Return to home beach
- Depth preference varies

### Baby Turtle Behavior

Baby turtles have unique behaviors:

- Rush to ocean after hatching
- Vulnerable to predators
- Drop scute when maturing

## Threats

Turtles and eggs face threats from:

- Zombies (stomp eggs)
- Zombified piglins (stomp eggs)
- Various predators (target babies)

## Scientific Basis

Based on sea turtle nesting behavior, including natal homing and beach imprinting.
